# Scenario Analysis — Local-Logistics Protocol

> Last updated: July 2026 | Three-Scenario P&L with Sensitivity Analysis

## Executive Summary

This document models three scenarios — **Best Case**, **Expected Case**, and **Worst Case** — each with a full 12-month P&L projection. Additionally, a sensitivity analysis quantifies the impact of key variable changes on profitability. The Expected Case projects a **Month 12 monthly revenue of £5,936** with a path to breakeven in **Q3 Year 2**.

## Sensitivity Analysis

### Variable 1: Platform Fee Percentage

| Fee % | Annual Platform Fee Rev (Exp.) | Change vs Base | Impact on Breakeven |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10% | £18,799 | -33% | +4 months |
| 12.5% | £23,498 | -17% | +2 months |
| **15% (Base)** | **£28,198** | **—** | **—** |
| 17.5% | £32,898 | +17% | -2 months |
| 20% | £37,597 | +33% | -3 months |

> **Finding:** Each 2.5% change in platform fee moves breakeven by ~2 months. The 15% rate is the sweet spot between competitiveness and viability.

### Variable 2: Courier Monthly Churn Rate

| Churn Rate | Avg Courier Lifespan | Courier LTV | LTV:CAC | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6% | 16.7 months | £324.48 | 6.8:1 | Excellent — reduces CAC pressure |
| 8% | 12.5 months | £243.00 | 5.1:1 | Strong |
| 10% | 10.0 months | £194.40 | 4.1:1 | Good |
| **12% (Base)** | **8.3 months** | **£161.33** | **3.4:1** | **Acceptable** |
| 15% | 6.7 months | £130.25 | 2.7:1 | ⚠️ Below target |
| 20% | 5.0 months | £97.20 | 2.0:1 | 🚨 Unsustainable |

> **Finding:** Courier retention is the single most impactful variable. Each 1% improvement in churn adds ~£15 to LTV. The Community Manager hire (M9) is critical infrastructure, not overhead.

### Variable 3: Merchant Monthly Churn Rate

| Churn Rate | Avg Lifespan | Merchant LTV | LTV:CAC | Annual SaaS NRR |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2% | 50 months | £3,563.50 | 5.6:1 | 130%+ |
| 3% | 33.3 months | £2,373.28 | 3.8:1 | 120% |
| **5% (Base)** | **20 months** | **£1,425.40** | **2.3:1** | **110%** |
| 8% | 12.5 months | £890.88 | 1.4:1 | 95% |
| 10% | 10.0 months | £712.70 | 1.1:1 | 85% |

> **Finding:** Merchant churn above 8%/month makes the B2B model unviable. Onboarding quality and first-30-day experience are make-or-break.

### Variable 4: Average Courier-Set Delivery Rate

| Avg Rate | Platform Fee/Drop | Annual Platform Rev | Gross Margin/Drop |
|---|---|---|---|
| £3.00 | £0.45 | £21,149 | 49.1% |
| £3.50 | £0.53 | £24,673 | 56.6% |
| **£4.00 (Base)** | **£0.60** | **£28,198** | **61.7%** |
| £4.50 | £0.68 | £31,723 | 65.6% |
| £5.00 | £0.75 | £35,248 | 68.7% |

> **Finding:** If competitive pressure drives average rates below £3.50, margin compression becomes concerning. The £2.50 rate floor protects against race-to-bottom.

### Variable 5: B2B Merchant Acquisition Rate

| New Merchants/Month | Merchants at M12 | Annual SaaS Revenue | Total Revenue Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1/month | 12 | £4,116 | -52% SaaS |
| 1.5/month | 18 | £6,174 | -28% SaaS |
| **2/month (Base)** | **26** | **£8,575** | **—** |
| 3/month | 36 | £12,348 | +44% SaaS |
| 5/month | 56 | £19,208 | +124% SaaS |

> **Finding:** B2B merchant acquisition has an outsized impact on revenue quality (recurring vs transactional). The field sales hire at M4 is the key enabler.

| Drops/day 150-300 (Expected Case) | Continue plan, optimise unit economics |
