# Persona Validation

## Local-Logistics Protocol — Customer & Courier Personas

> **Last Updated:** July 2026
> **Status:** Proto-Personas — To be validated and refined through customer discovery interviews

---

## Persona Development Methodology

These are **proto-personas** — educated hypotheses based on market research, analogous user studies (Deliveroo, Uber, TaskRabbit), and founder domain knowledge. They are explicitly *not* validated personas yet.

**Validation Process:**
1. Use these personas to guide interview recruitment (find real people matching each profile)
2. Conduct 8-10 interviews per persona archetype
3. Update personas with verbatim quotes, real demographics, and observed behaviours
4. Retire any persona that doesn't match reality; add new ones that emerge from interviews

## Sender Personas

### Persona S1: Adeel — The Overwhelmed Independent Pharmacist

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Adeel Hussain |
| **Age** | 47 |
| **Gender** | Male |
| **Location** | Marchmont Street, WC1 (Bloomsbury) |
| **Occupation** | Owner-pharmacist, Hussain's Pharmacy (independent, est. 2008) |
| **Annual Revenue** | £380,000 (pharmacy turnover) |
| **Personal Income** | £55,000 (after staff, rent, stock) |
| **Education** | MPharm, University of Bath |
| **Tech Savviness** | ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5) — Uses smartphone daily, WhatsApp, NHS Spine. Struggles with new apps. Needs clear UI. |
| **Marital Status** | Married, 2 children (ages 9 and 12) |

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Retain elderly and vulnerable patients who are switching to online pharmacies
- Stop leaving the dispensary counter to deliver prescriptions personally (currently does 3-5/week)
- Meet upcoming NHS delivery mandates without hiring a full-time driver
- Maintain CQC compliance for prescription delivery
- Eventually, offer delivery as a competitive advantage to attract *new* patients

**Frustrations:**
- "I lost Mrs. Chen last month. She's 82, can't walk to the shop, and her daughter signed her up for Pharmacy2U. That's £180/month in NHS contract income gone."
- "Last Tuesday I drove three prescriptions to homes after closing. By the time I got back, my wife was furious and my kids were already in bed."
- "I asked about DPD and they wanted £6.50 per parcel plus a contract. That's insane for a single prescription."
- "My locum won't do deliveries. He says it's not in his contract. He's right, but it means I have to do them all myself."

**Quote:**
> *"I became a pharmacist to help people, not to be a delivery driver. But if I don't deliver, I lose the patients who need me most."*

**A Day in Adeel's Life:**
- 07:30: Opens pharmacy, checks overnight prescriptions
- 08:00-12:00: Dispensing, patient consultations, MDS tray preparation
- 12:30: Drives two urgent prescriptions to housebound patients (45 min round trip)
- 13:15: Returns to find 3 patients waiting, phone ringing
- 13:15-18:00: Dispensing, stock management, NHS paperwork
- 18:30: One more delivery on the way home
- 19:15: Arrives home, exhausted

**Technology Stack:**
- Phone: Samsung Galaxy A54 (Android)
- POS: Cegedim pharmacy management system
- NHS: Connected to NHS Spine for electronic prescriptions
- Social: WhatsApp for pharmacy supplier groups

**Validation Method:**
- Walk into 15 independent pharmacies in WC1/N1 postcodes
- Ask to speak with the owner or superintendent pharmacist
- 20-minute semi-structured interview focused on delivery practices
- Observe current workflow (how do they handle delivery requests today?)
- Validate: Does Adeel's profile match reality? Are the pain points accurate?

### Persona S2: Priya — The Ambitious Vinted Power-Seller

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Priya Sharma |
| **Age** | 24 |
| **Gender** | Female |
| **Location** | Kentish Town, NW5 |
| **Occupation** | Marketing assistant (full-time) + Vinted/Depop reseller (side hustle) |
| **Annual Income** | £28,000 (job) + £6,000 (Vinted/Depop, net of costs) |
| **Education** | BA Marketing, University of Westminster (2023) |
| **Tech Savviness** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5) — Digital native. Uses 5+ marketplace apps. Can navigate any UX. |
| **Living Situation** | Shared flat with 2 housemates |

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Grow her reselling business to £15,000/year (enough to consider going full-time)
- Reduce time spent on shipping logistics (currently 3-4 hours/week packaging and posting)
- Offer faster delivery to get better ratings and more sales
- Avoid the post office queue (her nearest is a 15-minute walk with a consistent 10-minute queue)
- Sell more to local buyers without meeting strangers

**Frustrations:**
- "I spend every Saturday morning at the post office. It's my least favourite part of reselling."
- "A woman in Finsbury Park wanted my vintage Levi's. We arranged to meet at the tube station. She didn't show up. I wasted an hour."
- "Once a buyer claimed she never received the jacket. Royal Mail tracking just said 'delivered.' I lost £45 and a 5-star rating."
- "I'd sell more locally if I could just send things without meeting people. I had a weird experience meeting a guy from Gumtree and it put me off."

**Quote:**
> *"If I could send a package to someone 3 miles away for less than Royal Mail and it got there today? I'd use that every single time."*

**A Week in Priya's Reselling Life:**
- Monday-Friday: Full-time job. Lists 2-3 items on Vinted during lunch break.
- Evenings: Photographs new inventory, responds to buyers, packages sold items.
- Saturday AM: Post office run with 5-8 packages. 1.5 hours door-to-door.
- Saturday PM: Charity shop sourcing for inventory.
- Sunday: Photography, listings, accounting.

**Technology Stack:**
- Phone: iPhone 14 (iOS)
- Selling: Vinted, Depop, Facebook Marketplace, occasionally eBay
- Payments: Vinted wallet, PayPal, Monzo
- Social: Instagram, TikTok (for marketing her reselling)

**Validation Method:**
- Recruit via Vinted seller Facebook groups and Reddit r/VintedUK
- Screener survey: "Do you sell 5+ items/month? Are you based in London?"
- 30-minute video interview focused on shipping behaviour
- Ask to see their Vinted shipping history (with permission) to validate volume claims
- Validate: Is the post-office pain as intense as hypothesised? Is local delivery demand real?

### Persona S3: Martin — The Pragmatic Hardware Store Owner

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Martin Kowalski |
| **Age** | 56 |
| **Gender** | Male |
| **Location** | Caledonian Road, N1 |
| **Occupation** | Owner, Kowalski's Hardware & DIY (independent, est. 1995) |
| **Annual Revenue** | £220,000 (shop turnover) |
| **Personal Income** | £42,000 |
| **Education** | BTEC in Business Studies, City & Islington College |
| **Tech Savviness** | ⭐⭐ (2/5) — Uses Facebook for business page. Struggles with new technology. Wife manages the website. |

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Stop losing sales to Amazon and Screwfix
- Serve elderly customers who can't carry heavy items
- Compete on service, since he can't compete on price
- Keep the business viable for another 10 years until retirement

**Frustrations:**
- "A bloke came in for two bags of plaster. He didn't have a car. He said he'd come back. Never did. That's £30 gone."
- "I can't offer delivery. It's just me and one part-time lad. If I leave, the shop is empty."
- "My wife set up a website but nobody orders from it. They just check Amazon and it's there tomorrow."
- "Screwfix does free next-day. How can I compete with that?"

**Quote:**
> *"I've been here 30 years. I know every product in this shop. But knowledge doesn't matter if Amazon delivers it to their door and I can't."*

**Validation Method:**
- Walk into 15 hardware/DIY/print shops in N1/WC1 postcodes
- 15-minute conversation at the counter (don't take up too much of their time)
- Focus on: How often do they lose sales to delivery friction? Have they tried any solutions?
- Validate: Is delivery a top-3 concern? Is the tech barrier as high as assumed?

- GPS diary study: Ask 10 students to log their walking routes for 1 week
- Validate: Do students actually walk predictable routes? Is the £50 deposit a deal-breaker?

| **Age** | 31 |

| **Education** | BSc Computer Science, University of Lagos (degree not recognised in UK) |

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Maximise hourly earnings across all gig platforms
- Fill dead time between Deliveroo/Uber orders with package deliveries
- Build towards getting a UK-recognised qualification and full-time work
- Avoid being trapped in low-pay, high-control gig work

**Frustrations:**
- "Between 2-4pm, Deliveroo is dead. I just sit on my bike waiting. If I could do package drops, I'd earn an extra £20-30/week."
- "Uber cut my pay last month. Again. I have no say in what I earn."
- "I like that your platform lets me set my own price. That's the first time anyone's offered that."
- "The £50 deposit is fine. I know why it's there. The customer needs to trust me."

**Quote:**
> *"Every gig app promises freedom but they all control you. If this one actually lets me set my own rates, I'm interested."*

**Validation Method:**
- Recruit via gig worker forums (Reddit r/deliveroos, IWGB union networks), Facebook groups
- Screener: "Do you currently work on 2+ gig platforms in London?"
- 30-minute interview focused on earnings optimisation, dead time, multi-apping
- Track actual dead time: Ask 5 gig workers to screenshot their idle time between orders for 1 week
- Validate: Is dead time real and significant? Would they accept package drops during idle periods?
