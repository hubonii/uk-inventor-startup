## Journey Map 1: Sender (Merchant — Pharmacy Focus)

### Stage 1: Awareness

> *"I didn't know something like this existed"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Pharmacist loses another patient to Pharmacy2U, or spends 30 min delivering a prescription personally |
| **Touchpoints** | In-person visit from our BD rep, pharmacy trade press (Chemist+Druggist), local pharmacy network WhatsApp groups, NHS community pharmacy forum |
| **Actions** | Hears about the service, visits website or takes a flyer, asks "how does it work?" |
| **Thoughts** | "This sounds too good to be true. Who are these people? Is it safe for controlled drugs?" |
| **Emotions** | 😐 Skeptical → 🤔 Curious |
| **Pain Points** | No brand recognition. "Never heard of you." Trust deficit — handing prescriptions to strangers sounds risky. |
| **Opportunities** | Lead with the chain-of-custody protocol and CQC compliance angle. Show a 60-second demo video of the QR handshake. Offer a pharmacy-specific landing page with GPhC-relevant language. |

### Stage 2: Consideration

> *"Could this actually work for my pharmacy?"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Sees a demo, talks to another pharmacist who's trying it, or reads a case study |
| **Touchpoints** | Website/landing page, WhatsApp/phone call with our BD rep, demo walkthrough (in-person or video), FAQ document |
| **Actions** | Reviews pricing, asks about compliance, checks if their area is covered, discusses with staff |
| **Thoughts** | "How much does it cost per delivery? What happens if something goes wrong? Will my patients trust this?" |
| **Emotions** | 🤔 Evaluating → 😬 Anxious about compliance |
| **Pain Points** | Unclear pricing (needs simple, predictable cost structure). Compliance uncertainty (needs GPhC/CQC validation). Concern about courier reliability ("what if no one shows up?"). |
| **Opportunities** | Provide a pricing calculator ("you do X deliveries/day, it costs Y/month"). Prepare a compliance one-pager signed by a pharmacy consultant. Offer a "first 10 deliveries free" trial to derisk. |

### Stage 3: Signup

> *"OK, let's try it"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Free trial offer, competitive pressure from nearby pharmacy that's already using it, or a specific incident ("I lost Mrs. Ahmed today because she switched to Echo") |
| **Touchpoints** | Signup form (web or in-app), onboarding call with BD rep, installation of dispatch tablet/app, staff training session |
| **Actions** | Creates merchant account, adds pharmacy details, sets delivery radius, trains 1-2 staff on QR dispatch flow |
| **Thoughts** | "This better be easy to use. My technicians aren't tech-savvy. I don't have time for complicated training." |
| **Emotions** | 😤 Impatient (wants it done fast) → 😊 Relieved (if onboarding is smooth) |
| **Pain Points** | Lengthy signup forms. Need to input pharmacy details, opening hours, delivery zone. Staff resistance to change. IT friction if they need to install software. |
| **Opportunities** | Pre-fill pharmacy details from GPhC register. Offer white-glove onboarding (our rep sets everything up in 15 minutes at their counter). Single-page staff quick-start card (laminated, sticks to the counter). |

### Stage 4: First Use

> *"Moment of truth"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | First patient needs a delivery. Pharmacist decides to use the platform instead of driving it themselves. |
| **Touchpoints** | App dispatch screen, courier matching notification, real-time tracking, patient receiving QR handshake, delivery confirmation |
| **Actions** | Creates delivery request, hands package to courier (scans QR), watches tracking, receives confirmation |
| **Thoughts** | "Will a courier actually show up? How long will it take? Will the patient get their meds? What if something goes wrong?" |
| **Emotions** | 😰 Nervous → 😲 Surprised (if it works) → 😊 Relieved → 😄 Delighted |
| **Pain Points** | Long wait for first courier (if supply is thin in early days). Uncertainty about courier identity. Patient unfamiliarity with QR handshake process. First delivery taking longer than expected. |
| **Opportunities** | **THIS IS THE MAKE-OR-BREAK MOMENT.** Guarantee first delivery with a standby courier (even if we subsidise it). Send a real-time SMS to the patient explaining what to expect. Follow up with the pharmacist within 1 hour to ask how it went. |

### Stage 5: Regular Use

> *"This is now part of how we operate"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | 5+ successful deliveries. Staff comfortable with the flow. Patients asking for delivery proactively. |
| **Touchpoints** | Daily dispatch dashboard, weekly invoice, delivery analytics, courier rating system, patient feedback |
| **Actions** | Dispatches 5-15 deliveries/day, reviews weekly invoice, manages preferred courier list, handles occasional issues |
| **Thoughts** | "This is saving me 2 hours/day. My patients love it. But I need the billing to be easier and I want to see delivery stats." |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Satisfied → 🤓 Wants more features |
| **Pain Points** | Invoice reconciliation (needs to match to patient records). Occasional courier no-shows. Want to schedule recurring deliveries for chronic patients. Need better reporting for CQC audits. |
| **Opportunities** | Build automated billing integration with pharmacy PMR systems. Add "preferred courier" feature. Create CQC-ready delivery audit reports. Introduce scheduled/recurring deliveries for repeat prescriptions. |

### Stage 6: Advocacy

> *"I'm telling other pharmacists about this"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Visible business impact (retained patients, time saved, revenue growth). Another pharmacist asks how they offer delivery. |
| **Touchpoints** | Pharmacy network events, WhatsApp groups, NPA/LPC meetings, Chemist+Druggist mentions, referral program |
| **Actions** | Recommends to peers, provides a testimonial, refers other pharmacies, speaks at a local pharmacy network meeting |
| **Thoughts** | "This genuinely improved my business. I want to help other pharmacists discover it — and I wouldn't mind a referral bonus." |
| **Emotions** | 😄 Proud → 🤩 Evangelical |
| **Pain Points** | No structured referral program. No easy way to share ("just tell them to Google us" isn't good enough). Pharmacist doesn't want to seem like they're "selling" something. |
| **Opportunities** | Launch a pharmacy referral program (£100 credit per successful referral). Create a "case study" one-pager with the pharmacy's story (anonymised if preferred). Provide shareable content for WhatsApp groups. Invite them to be an "Ambassador Pharmacy" with a badge for their window. |

## Journey Map 2: Sender (C2C — Vinted/Depop Seller)

| **Trigger** | Sells an item on Vinted to someone in the same city. Frustrated by Royal Mail queue or delivery cost. |

| **Emotions** | 😐 Unaware → 🤔 "Wait, there's a local delivery option?" |
| **Opportunities** | Target Vinted/Depop seller hashtags on social media. Partner with reseller influencers. Post in seller community forums. |

### Stage 2: Consideration

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Sees a specific ad comparing our £3 price vs. Royal Mail £3.49 + 2-day wait |
| **Touchpoints** | Landing page, app store listing, TikTok demo video, Trustpilot reviews |
| **Emotions** | 🤔 Comparing → 😊 "This could save me time and money" |
| **Opportunities** | Price comparison calculator ("you ship 10 items/week, save £X/month"). Social proof from other Vinted sellers. |

### Stage 3: Signup

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Downloads app, completes signup in <2 minutes |
| **Touchpoints** | App store, signup flow (phone number, address, payment) |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Easy and fast → or → 😤 "Too many steps, I'll do it later" |
| **Opportunities** | Apple/Google sign-in. Skip payment until first delivery. Request only essential info upfront. |

### Stage 4: First Use

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Makes a local sale, decides to try the platform instead of Royal Mail |
| **Touchpoints** | Delivery creation screen, courier match, tracking, buyer receives QR handshake |
| **Emotions** | 😰 "Will this actually work?" → 😲 "The courier came in 12 minutes!" → 😄 "The buyer is happy!" |
| **Opportunities** | First delivery free or discounted. In-app walkthrough. Immediate buyer feedback. |

### Stage 5: Regular Use

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | 3+ successful deliveries. Starts using it for every local sale. |
| **Touchpoints** | App, delivery history, earnings tracker (if they also courier), rating system |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Habitual → 🤓 "I want batch shipping" |
| **Opportunities** | Batch delivery feature for high-volume sellers. Integration with Vinted/Depop APIs (auto-detect local sales). |

### Stage 6: Advocacy

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Tells other sellers. Posts about it on seller forums. |
| **Touchpoints** | Social media, seller communities, referral link |
| **Emotions** | 🤩 "This is a life hack for Vinted sellers" |
| **Opportunities** | Referral credits. "Share your delivery stats" social templates. Seller community ambassador program. |

## Journey Map 3: Courier

### Stage 1: Awareness

> *"I could get paid to walk?"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Student sees a poster in halls, gig worker sees an ad, local resident sees a social post |
| **Touchpoints** | University poster boards, Instagram ads, word of mouth, Freshers' Fair booth, gig worker forums |
| **Actions** | Sees the value prop ("earn £3-8 per delivery on walks you're already taking"), clicks to learn more |
| **Thoughts** | "Is this legit? How much can I actually earn? Is it safe? Why do I need to put down £50?" |
| **Emotions** | 🤔 Curious but wary |
| **Pain Points** | £50 deposit is a barrier. "Sounds like a scam." Comparison to Deliveroo (which doesn't require a deposit). |
| **Opportunities** | Emphasise that £50 is refundable and earnable-back in 5-8 deliveries. Use testimonials from early couriers. Show transparent earnings data. |

### Stage 2: Consideration

> *"Let me check the numbers"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Reads earnings page, talks to a friend who's doing it, checks reviews |
| **Touchpoints** | Website earnings calculator, FAQ, courier stories, Reddit/student forums |
| **Actions** | Calculates potential earnings, checks delivery zones, reads T&Cs, compares to Deliveroo/UberEats |
| **Thoughts** | "£50 deposit is steep but I get it back. £4 per drop × 3 drops/hour = £12/hr. That's not bad for walking." |
| **Emotions** | 🤔 Calculating → 😊 "The maths works" |
| **Pain Points** | £50 upfront feels risky. Unclear how many deliveries are available. No guaranteed earnings. |
| **Opportunities** | "Earnings guarantee" for first week (min £X if you're available for Y hours). Deposit installment plan (£10/week for 5 weeks). Real-time demand heatmap so couriers can see where deliveries are. |

### Stage 3: Signup

> *"OK, I'm in — but this ID check better be fast"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Decides the economics work. Downloads app. |
| **Touchpoints** | App download, signup flow (ID verification, selfie, address, bank details, £50 deposit) |
| **Actions** | Completes identity verification, pays £50 deposit, sets availability and delivery zone |
| **Thoughts** | "This ID check is more thorough than Deliveroo's. The £50 stings but I understand why. When can I start?" |
| **Emotions** | 😤 Impatient with verification → 😊 Approved → 😰 £50 leaves account |
| **Pain Points** | ID verification can take 24-48 hours (Onfido/Veriff). £50 deposit creates checkout abandonment. Bank details entry feels invasive. |
| **Opportunities** | Instant ID verification (Onfido Real ID can do <2 min). Show the deposit balance in-app prominently. Activate courier immediately with a "first delivery" tutorial. |

### Stage 4: First Delivery

> *"Am I doing this right?"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | First delivery request appears. Courier accepts. |
| **Touchpoints** | In-app delivery alert, pickup navigation, sender interaction (QR scan), delivery navigation, recipient interaction (QR scan), earnings credited |
| **Actions** | Accepts job → navigates to pickup → scans QR with sender → carries package → navigates to dropoff → scans QR with recipient → gets paid |
| **Thoughts** | "Where exactly is the pickup? Do I just walk in? What do I say? How does the QR thing work? Did I do it right?" |
| **Emotions** | 😰 Anxious → 😊 "That was easy!" → 😄 "+£4.00 in my wallet!" |
| **Pain Points** | Ambiguous pickup instructions ("the pharmacy on the high street" — which one?). Awkward first interaction with sender. QR scanning confusion (hold phone here? which screen?). Navigation to recipient address issues. |
| **Opportunities** | **THIS IS THE MAKE-OR-BREAK MOMENT.** Provide step-by-step in-app walkthrough for first delivery. Include sender's photo and exact shopfront image. QR scanning tutorial before first pickup. Celebratory screen after first successful delivery with earnings shown. |

### Stage 5: Regular Use

> *"This is my side hustle now"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | 5+ deliveries completed. Courier has a rhythm. |
| **Touchpoints** | Daily earnings dashboard, delivery history, availability calendar, route preferences, support chat |
| **Actions** | Sets daily availability, picks optimal routes, builds relationships with regular senders, tracks weekly earnings |
| **Thoughts** | "I'm averaging £11/hr. I want more deliveries in my area. Can I set favourite pickup spots?" |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Confident and earning → 🤓 Wants optimisation tools |
| **Pain Points** | Uneven delivery distribution (some hours busy, some dead). Want to set preferred merchants. Occasional difficult recipients. Deposit still locked up. |
| **Opportunities** | "Hot zones" heatmap showing demand density. Preferred merchant matching. Progressive deposit return (£10 back after every 20 successful deliveries). Weekly earnings report with tips to optimise. |

### Stage 6: Advocacy

> *"You should sign up — I'm earning £50/week just walking to uni"*

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Friends ask about the extra income. Courier posts earnings on social media. |
| **Touchpoints** | Word of mouth, social media earnings screenshots, referral link, university society |
| **Actions** | Refers friends, shares earnings stats, answers questions about the experience |
| **Thoughts** | "I genuinely enjoy this and I want my friends to join — plus I get a referral bonus." |
| **Emotions** | 🤩 Proud and evangelical |
| **Pain Points** | No referral incentive. No easy way to share earnings (privacy concerns about screenshots). |
| **Opportunities** | Referral bonus (£5 credit per referred courier who completes 5 deliveries). In-app "share your stats" with privacy-controlled shareable cards. Courier leaderboard (opt-in) to gamify. |

## Emotional Journey Summary

### Sender Emotional Arc

```
    😄 Delighted ─────────────────────────────────── ★ Advocacy
    😊 Satisfied ──────────────────── ★ Regular Use
    😲 Surprised ─────── ★ First Use (it worked!)
    😐 Neutral ──── ★ Signup
    🤔 Curious ─ ★ Consideration
    😐 Unaware ★ Awareness
    😰 Anxious ─────── ★ First Use (will it work?)
    😤 Frustrated ── ★ Signup (if friction)
```

### Courier Emotional Arc

```
    🤩 Evangelical ────────────────────────────── ★ Advocacy
    😊 Confident ──────────────────── ★ Regular Use
    😄 Excited ──────── ★ First delivery done!
    😰 Nervous ─────── ★ First pickup
    😤 Impatient ─── ★ Signup (ID + deposit)
    🤔 Calculating ─ ★ Consideration
    😐 Curious ─── ★ Awareness
```

## Critical Moments of Truth

| Moment | Stakeholder | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **First courier pickup** | Courier + Sender | Awkward interaction, QR confusion | Step-by-step in-app guide, sender photo, practice mode |
| **First delivery confirmation** | Sender + Recipient | Recipient confused by QR handshake | Pre-delivery SMS to recipient explaining the process |
| **Courier no-show** | Sender | Lost trust, won't use again | Backup courier system, proactive re-matching, sender notification |
| **£50 deposit payment** | Courier | Signup abandonment | Instalment plan, emphasise refundability, show earnings potential |
| **First weekly invoice** | Merchant | Unexpected charges, reconciliation confusion | Clear invoice format, itemised per-delivery breakdown |
| **First complaint/dispute** | All | Platform trust collapse if handled poorly | 15-minute response SLA, automatic refund for first dispute, human support |
