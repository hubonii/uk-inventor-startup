# Customer Discovery Interview Questions

## Local-Logistics Protocol — 60+ Question Bank

> **Last Updated:** July 2026
> **Methodology:** Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick) — Focus on past behaviour, not hypotheticals
> **Rule:** Never pitch. Never ask "would you use this?" Ask about their life, not your idea.

---

## Category 1: Problem Discovery (12 Questions)

### Opening & Context

**Q1.** Tell me about your [business/selling routine/daily commute]. Walk me through a typical day.
- *Probing follow-up:* What's the most frustrating part of your day?

**Q2.** When was the last time you needed to get something delivered locally — like within a few miles?
- *Probing follow-up:* Tell me exactly what happened. What did you do?

**Q3.** How often does this come up? Once a week? Once a day? More?
- *Probing follow-up:* Can you show me in your records/order history?

### Problem Exploration

**Q4.** What's the hardest part about getting things delivered locally?
- *Probing follow-up:* Why is that hard? What makes it specifically difficult for you?

**Q5.** Tell me about a time when a delivery went wrong. What happened?
- *Probing follow-up:* How did you handle it? What did it cost you (time, money, stress)?

**Q6.** Have you ever lost a [customer/sale/patient] because you couldn't deliver something?
- *Probing follow-up:* How often does this happen? Can you give me a specific example?

**Q7.** [For merchants] If a customer needs something delivered today, what happens right now?
- *Probing follow-up:* Walk me through the steps. How long does it take? Who does it?

**Q8.** What's the single biggest problem in your [business/selling routine] that you haven't been able to solve?
- *Probing follow-up:* How long has this been a problem? What have you tried?

### Urgency & Importance

**Q9.** On a scale of 1-10, how painful is this delivery problem for you? (1 = minor annoyance, 10 = keeping you up at night)
- *Probing follow-up:* What would make it a 10?

**Q10.** If this problem disappeared tomorrow, what would change for your [business/life]?
- *Probing follow-up:* Can you put a number on that? (Time saved per week? Revenue gained?)

**Q11.** Is this problem getting worse, staying the same, or getting better over time?
- *Probing follow-up:* What's making it worse?

**Q12.** Have you talked to other [pharmacists/shop owners/sellers] about this problem? What do they say?
- *Probing follow-up:* Is everyone struggling with the same thing, or is it just you?

## Category 2: Current Behaviour (10 Questions)

**Q13.** Walk me through exactly how you handled your last local delivery. Step by step.
- *Probing follow-up:* How long did each step take?

**Q14.** What do you currently use to send/deliver things locally? (Royal Mail? Uber? Drive yourself? Ask someone?)
- *Probing follow-up:* Why that option? Have you tried anything else?

**Q15.** How much do you currently spend on local delivery per week/month?
- *Probing follow-up:* Is that within budget or does it feel too high?

**Q16.** How much time do you spend on delivery-related tasks per week?
- *Probing follow-up:* What else could you be doing with that time?

**Q17.** Have you tried any apps or services for local delivery? (Uber Connect, Stuart, Gophr, TaskRabbit?)
- *Probing follow-up:* What happened? Why did you stop using it / keep using it?

**Q18.** [For C2C] When you sell locally on Vinted/Depop, how do you handle the handover?
- *Probing follow-up:* Have you ever had a bad experience meeting a buyer/seller?

**Q19.** [For pharmacies] Do you currently deliver prescriptions? If so, how?
- *Probing follow-up:* Who does the delivery? How do you record proof of delivery?

**Q20.** How do you decide whether to offer delivery on a particular order?
- *Probing follow-up:* What's the threshold? (Distance? Item value? Customer relationship?)

**Q21.** [For couriers/potential couriers] Have you ever done gig work? (Deliveroo, Uber, Amazon Flex?)
- *Probing follow-up:* What did you like? What did you hate?

**Q22.** [For students] When you need something brought to you from across campus, what do you do?
- *Probing follow-up:* How often does this happen? What's the workaround?

## Category 3: Pain Points (10 Questions)

**Q23.** What's the worst thing about [current delivery method]?
- *Probing follow-up:* Have you complained about this to anyone? What did they say?

**Q24.** Tell me about the last time you were really frustrated with a delivery.
- *Probing follow-up:* What specifically frustrated you? What would have made it okay?

**Q25.** What keeps you up at night about your [business/selling/delivery process]?
- *Probing follow-up:* Why? What's the worst-case scenario?

**Q26.** If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about local delivery, what would it be?
- *Probing follow-up:* Why that specifically? What would it change?

**Q27.** [For merchants] Have you ever had a delivery dispute? (Item lost, wrong address, damage?)
- *Probing follow-up:* How did you resolve it? What did it cost?

**Q28.** [For merchants] Do you worry about liability when someone else delivers your products?
- *Probing follow-up:* What's your biggest concern? (Theft? Damage? Customer complaint? Regulatory?)

**Q29.** [For C2C] Have you ever avoided a sale because you didn't want to deal with the delivery?
- *Probing follow-up:* How often? What was the item? How much was it worth?

**Q30.** How do you feel about handing a valuable item to someone you don't know?
- *Probing follow-up:* What would make you feel safe doing that?

**Q31.** [For couriers] What's the biggest downside of gig work right now?
- *Probing follow-up:* Is pay the main issue, or is it something else?

**Q32.** [For couriers] How do you protect yourself from false claims? (e.g., customer says you didn't deliver)
- *Probing follow-up:* Has this happened to you? What did you do?

## Category 4: Willingness to Pay (8 Questions)

**Q33.** If a reliable courier could come to your [shop/flat] in 10 minutes and deliver something locally, what would you expect to pay?
- *Probing follow-up:* Why that number? What are you comparing it to?

**Q34.** At what price would local same-day delivery feel like a bargain?
- *Probing follow-up:* What would you expect at that price? (Speed? Tracking? Insurance?)

**Q35.** At what price would it feel too expensive and you'd rather do it yourself?
- *Probing follow-up:* What's your breakeven? (Time cost? Opportunity cost?)

**Q36.** At what price would it feel so cheap that you'd question the quality?
- *Probing follow-up:* What would you worry about?

**Q37.** At what price would it feel expensive but you'd still pay it?
- *Probing follow-up:* What would it need to include at that price?

**Q38.** [For merchants] Would you prefer to pay per delivery or a monthly subscription?
- *Probing follow-up:* Why? How much per month would you consider fair?

**Q39.** [For merchants] Would you pass the delivery cost to your customer, absorb it, or split it?
- *Probing follow-up:* What do your competitors do?

**Q40.** [For couriers] How much would you need to earn per delivery for it to be worth your time?
- *Probing follow-up:* What if the delivery was on a route you were already walking? Would that change the number?

## Category 5: Feature Validation (12 Questions)

### Chain of Custody / Trust

**Q41.** If a courier carried your package, how would you want to confirm it was delivered?
- *Probing follow-up:* What would give you the most confidence? (Photo? Signature? Code? GPS?)

**Q42.** How would you feel if both the sender and receiver had to scan a QR code to confirm the handover?
- *Probing follow-up:* Does that feel like too much friction or just right?

**Q43.** If the courier put down a £50 refundable deposit, would that change how much you trust them?
- *Probing follow-up:* Is £50 enough? Too much? Too little?

### Real-Time Tracking

**Q44.** How important is it for you to track the package in real-time?
- *Probing follow-up:* Would you check it constantly or just want it available?

**Q45.** [For merchants] Would you want your customer to see the courier's location too?
- *Probing follow-up:* Would that reduce the number of "where's my delivery?" calls?

### Courier Pricing

**Q46.** [For couriers] If you could set your own delivery price, what would you set it at?
- *Probing follow-up:* Would it change based on distance? Weight? Time of day?

**Q47.** [For senders] If couriers set their own prices and you could choose between a cheaper/slower courier and a faster/more expensive one, how would you decide?
- *Probing follow-up:* What's the max you'd pay for speed?

### Scheduling & On-Demand

**Q48.** Would you want to schedule deliveries in advance, or is it always last-minute?
- *Probing follow-up:* What's the split? (% scheduled vs. % on-demand)

**Q49.** What's the longest you'd be willing to wait for a courier after requesting one?
- *Probing follow-up:* What if it were cheaper to wait longer?

### App & Technology

**Q50.** How would you want to request a delivery? (App? Website? WhatsApp? Phone call?)
- *Probing follow-up:* Why that channel? What do you use for other services?

**Q51.** [For merchants] Would you want a dashboard to see all your deliveries, costs, and reports?
- *Probing follow-up:* What information is most important to you?

**Q52.** [For merchants] Would integration with your existing system (POS, pharmacy software) be important?
- *Probing follow-up:* What system do you use? What would you want integrated?

## Category 6: Trust & Safety (10 Questions)

**Q53.** What's the biggest thing that would prevent you from using a service where strangers carry your packages?
- *Probing follow-up:* What would it take to overcome that?

**Q54.** Have you ever had something stolen during delivery? What happened?
- *Probing follow-up:* How did you get compensation? How long did it take?

**Q55.** [For couriers] Have you ever felt unsafe during a delivery? What happened?
- *Probing follow-up:* What would make you feel safer?

**Q56.** [For couriers] How would you feel about putting down a £50 refundable deposit to show senders you're trustworthy?
- *Probing follow-up:* Is £50 reasonable? At what amount would you refuse?

**Q57.** What kind of background check would you expect a delivery courier to have?
- *Probing follow-up:* Would a verified ID, selfie match, and £50 deposit be enough? Or would you need a DBS check?

**Q58.** [For pharmacies] What documentation do you need for a CQC audit about delivery?
- *Probing follow-up:* Could a digital timestamp + GPS + QR confirmation satisfy that?

**Q59.** If something went wrong (package damaged, lost, or stolen), how would you expect it to be resolved?
- *Probing follow-up:* How fast? Full refund? Replacement? What's acceptable?

**Q60.** How do you feel about the courier knowing your address?
- *Probing follow-up:* Would you want the option to meet at a nearby point instead?

**Q61.** [For couriers] How would you protect yourself from being falsely accused?
- *Probing follow-up:* Would a QR handshake (where the recipient confirms receipt) protect you?

**Q62.** Would you use a delivery service differently depending on the value of the item? (e.g., a £5 item vs. a £200 item)
- *Probing follow-up:* At what value threshold would you stop using a peer courier?

---

## Bonus: Competitive & Market Questions (5 Questions)

**Q63.** Have you heard of [Uber Connect / Stuart / Gophr / Quiqup]?
- *Probing follow-up:* Have you used them? What was your experience?

**Q64.** What would a competitor have to do to win your loyalty?
- *Probing follow-up:* Is it price? Speed? Trust? Convenience?

**Q65.** If Amazon started offering £3 same-day local delivery for any item, would that change things for you?
- *Probing follow-up:* What would be the impact on your business/selling?

**Q66.** [For merchants] Have any of your competitors started offering delivery?
- *Probing follow-up:* How? What impact has it had?

**Q67.** What's the one thing that would make you tell a friend about a local delivery service?
- *Probing follow-up:* What would make you stop using it?

## Key "Mom Test" Reminders

1. **Talk about THEIR life, not YOUR idea.** Bad: "Would you use an app that...?" Good: "Tell me about the last time you needed to deliver something."

2. **Ask about the PAST, not the FUTURE.** Bad: "Would you pay £4 for delivery?" Good: "How much did you pay last time?"

3. **Get commitment or advancement.** End with: "Can I follow up with you in 2 weeks?" or "Would you be willing to try a prototype?" A "yes" here is real signal. A polite "sure, maybe" is not.

4. **Shut up and listen.** The 80/20 rule: They talk 80%, you talk 20%.

5. **Awkward silence is your friend.** After they answer, count to 5 before speaking. They'll often fill the silence with the most honest thing they say all interview.
