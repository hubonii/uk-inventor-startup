# Customer Discovery Strategy

## Local-Logistics Protocol — How We Learn Before We Build

> **Last Updated:** July 2026
> **Duration:** 10 weeks total (8-week sprint + 2-week analysis)
> **Budget:** £2,500
> **Methodology:** Lean Startup + Mom Test + Design Thinking

---

## Strategic Philosophy

> "Get out of the building." — Steve Blank

Our discovery strategy is built on three principles:

1. **Face-to-face first.** Our beachhead is local merchants on a specific high street. We walk in and talk to them. No cold emails. No LinkedIn outreach. Door-to-door, founder-led sales-as-research.
2. **Behaviour over opinion.** We don't ask "would you use this?" We observe what they currently do, measure the pain, and test whether our solution changes behaviour.
3. **Density over breadth.** We research deeply within the University Wedge (UCL/Bloomsbury, WC1/N1) rather than thinly across all of London. If it doesn't work here, it won't work anywhere.

## Discovery Channels

### Channel 1: High-Street Walk-Ins (Primary)

**Target:** Independent pharmacies, hardware stores, print shops, specialist retailers in WC1/N1

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Walk into shops, ask to speak with the owner, conduct a 15-20 minute interview |
| **Geographic Focus** | Marchmont Street, Lamb's Conduit Street, Caledonian Road, Chapel Market, Fonthill Road, Seven Sisters Road (first mile) |
| **Best Times** | Tuesday-Thursday, 2:00-4:00pm (quiet period, owner more likely to chat) |
| **Target** | 30 shops visited → 25 merchant interviews (15 pharmacies + 10 hardware/print) |
| **Materials** | Business card, 1-page research brief (who we are, why we're asking), notebook |
| **Cost** | £50 (transport) |

**Walk-In Protocol:**
1. Enter shop. Wait if busy. Don't interrupt a customer transaction.
2. Approach the counter: "Hi, are you the owner? I'm [Name], I'm researching delivery challenges for local shops. I'm not selling anything — I'd love to ask you a few questions. Takes about 15 minutes."
3. If no: "No problem, thank you. Is there a better time to come back?"
4. If yes: Begin interview. Stand at the counter (don't ask them to sit down — this is their workspace).
5. After: Leave a business card. Ask if you can follow up.

## Methods Matrix

| Method | Purpose | Sample Size | When | Output |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Semi-Structured Interviews** | Deep problem understanding, behavioural insight | 50 | Weeks 2-7 | Transcripts, quotes, pain scores, WTP signals |
| **Observation** | Watch actual delivery behaviour, identify friction | 5-10 sessions | Weeks 2-4 | Field notes, process maps, time measurements |
| **Walking Diary Study** | Map student walking routes for courier supply modelling | 10 students, 1 week each | Week 5-6 | GPS route data, frequency patterns, timing |
| **Screener Survey** | Recruit and pre-qualify C2C sellers | 50-100 responses | Week 3 (launched) | Qualified leads for interviews |
| **Competitive Shopping** | Experience existing alternatives firsthand | 5 transactions | Week 1-2 | Cost, time, UX, trust assessment per competitor |
| **Expert Consultation** | Validate regulatory and industry assumptions | 3-5 | Weeks 2-6 | Compliance requirements, industry context |

### Observation Protocol

For 5-10 sessions, we will silently observe actual delivery situations:

1. **Pharmacy self-delivery:** Shadow a pharmacist doing a delivery run. Time each step. Note friction.
2. **Post office visit:** Stand in a post office during peak hours. Time queue. Observe Vinted/Depop shippers (they're usually obvious — brown packaging, multiple parcels).
3. **C2C meet-up:** Observe (from a safe distance) a Facebook Marketplace/Gumtree handover. Note the social dynamics, location choice, time spent.
4. **Student item exchange:** Observe students exchanging textbooks/items on campus. How do they coordinate? Where do they meet?

### Competitive Shopping

Before interviewing customers, we need to experience the pain ourselves:

| Competitor | Experiment | Expected Insight |
|---|---|---|
| **Royal Mail** | Ship a small package to an address 2 miles away | Time cost (queue + processing), cash cost, tracking quality |
| **Uber Connect** | Send a package via Uber Connect in central London | Cost, speed, courier experience, proof of delivery |
| **InPost** | Use a locker-to-locker shipment | Convenience, limitations, cost |
| **Meeting a stranger** | List an item on Facebook Marketplace, arrange a local meetup | Safety feeling, coordination effort, time cost |
| **Self-delivery** | Walk a package 1.5 miles across Bloomsbury | Time cost, effort, opportunity cost |

## Success Criteria & Kill Criteria

### Discovery is SUCCESSFUL if:

| Criterion | Threshold | Measured By |
|---|---|---|
| Pharmacies confirm delivery pain | ≥60% rate pain ≥7/10 | Interview pain scores |
| Merchants confirm lost sales from no delivery | ≥50% report ≥2 lost sales/week | Interview responses |
| WTP aligns with our pricing | ≥50% accept £3.50-5.50/drop | Van Westendorp pre-signals |
| Couriers willing to supply at target earnings | ≥60% would courier for £4-5/drop | Interview responses |
| £50 deposit is not a deal-breaker | ≥50% of potential couriers accept it | Interview responses |
| Trust in QR handshake concept | ≥70% find it reassuring | Interview responses |

### Discovery KILLS the idea if:

| Kill Criterion | Threshold | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Pain is not real | <30% rate pain ≥7/10 | Problem doesn't exist at sufficient intensity |
| No one will pay | <30% accept our target pricing | Unit economics don't work |
| Couriers won't supply | <30% willing to courier at our rates | No supply side = no marketplace |
| £50 deposit is universally rejected | <20% accept the deposit | Core trust mechanism fails |
| Regulatory blocker | GPhC or CQC says QR proof is insufficient | Need to redesign the trust protocol |
| Demand density too low | <50 drops/day modelled in the wedge | Marketplace won't reach liquidity |

### Data Privacy

- All interview data stored on encrypted Google Drive
- Participant names anonymised in analysis (use persona codes: S1-01, S2-01, etc.)
- Audio recordings deleted after transcription unless participant consents to retention
- GDPR compliance: verbal consent at start of each interview, right to withdraw
- No data shared with third parties
