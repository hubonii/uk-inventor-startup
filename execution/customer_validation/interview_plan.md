> **Last Updated:** July 2026

> **Target:** 50+ interviews across 6 persona archetypes

## Objectives

1. **Validate or invalidate** the 4 customer segment hypotheses and 6 persona archetypes
2. **Quantify pain intensity** — confirm severity rankings from [pain_point_analysis.md](pain_point_analysis.md)
3. **Test willingness to pay** — initial pricing signals before formal Van Westendorp study
4. **Discover unknown unknowns** — pains, jobs, and segments we haven't considered
5. **Collect founder-market-fit stories** for investor conversations

## Recruitment Strategy

### Segment A: Independent Pharmacies (15 interviews)

**Method: Cold Walk-In + Warm Introduction**

| Channel | Approach | Expected Yield |
|---|---|---|
| Cold walk-ins | Visit 25 pharmacies in WC1/N1/NW1 postcodes. Ask to speak with the owner or superintendent. Pitch: "I'm researching local delivery challenges for pharmacies — can I ask you 5 quick questions?" | 40-50% agree → 10-12 interviews |
| LPC Network | Contact Camden & Islington Local Pharmaceutical Committee. Ask for introduction to members. | 3-5 referrals |
| Pharmacy conference | Attend NPA local event or Chemist+Druggist networking evening. | 2-3 warm introductions |

**Screening Criteria:**
- ✅ Independent pharmacy (not Boots, Lloyds, or franchise chain)
- ✅ Located in WC1, N1, NW1, or E1 postcode
- ✅ Owner or superintendent pharmacist available to speak
- ✅ Currently does OR has considered offering delivery
- ❌ Exclude pharmacies with existing courier contracts (they've already solved the problem)

**Recruitment Script:**
> "Hi, I'm [Name]. I'm a founder building a same-day delivery solution specifically for independent pharmacies. I'm not selling anything today — I just want to understand how pharmacies currently handle deliveries and what the challenges are. Could I ask you a few questions? It'll take about 15 minutes."

### Segment C: C2C Resellers (8 interviews)

| Channel | Approach | Expected Yield |
|---|---|---|

| Facebook Groups | "London Vinted Sellers," "Depop UK Community" — same post | 5-10 responses |
| Instagram DMs | Search #VintedUK #DepopSeller London geotag. DM high-volume sellers. | 3-5 responses |

**Screening Criteria:**
- ✅ Based in London (ideally North/Central)
- ✅ Sells 5+ items/month on Vinted, Depop, or similar
- ✅ Has made at least one local (same-city) sale
- ❌ Exclude sellers who only ship nationally

**Screener Survey (Google Form):**
1. Which platforms do you sell on? (Vinted / Depop / eBay / FB Marketplace / Other)
2. How many items did you sell last month?
3. Have you ever sold to someone in the same city? (Y/N)
4. How do you currently ship local sales? (Royal Mail / Meet in person / Other)
5. What's your London postcode?
6. Would you be available for a 30-minute Zoom call in exchange for a £15 Amazon voucher? (Y/N)

## Interview Protocol

### Before the Interview

1. **Confirm logistics:** Location, time, participant name
2. **Review persona hypothesis** for this participant type
3. **Prepare materials:** Notebook, phone (for recording, with permission), interview guide
4. **Mindset check:** We are here to LEARN, not PITCH. No product demos. No feature discussions unless initiated by the participant.

### During the Interview

**Opening (2 minutes):**
> "Thank you for your time. I'm researching local delivery challenges. I'm not selling anything — I just want to understand your experience. There are no right or wrong answers. Can I record this for my notes? Nothing will be shared publicly."

**Structure:**
1. Warm-up / Context (3 min)
2. Problem Discovery (5-8 min)
3. Current Behaviour (5 min)
4. Pain Points (5 min)
5. Willingness to Pay (3 min)
6. Wrap-Up (2 min)

**Rules:**
- ❌ Never pitch the product
- ❌ Never ask "would you use this?" (hypothetical questions get hypothetical answers)
- ❌ Never argue with their perspective
- ✅ Ask "why?" at least 3 times per topic (Five Whys technique)
- ✅ Ask for specific examples ("tell me about the last time...")
- ✅ Note emotional language ("frustrated," "scared," "annoying")
- ✅ Note what they DO, not just what they SAY

### After the Interview

1. **Within 1 hour:** Write up key quotes, observations, and surprises
2. **Tag themes:** Pain level (1-10), willingness to pay (£X), trust concerns, feature requests
3. **Update tracking spreadsheet**
4. **Debrief with co-founder** (if applicable) — what did we learn? What should we change?

## Analysis Framework

### Quantitative Analysis

After completing all 50 interviews, we will:

1. **Pain Frequency Matrix:** Count how many interviewees in each segment mentioned each pain point
2. **Pain Severity Distribution:** Plot the distribution of pain scores (1-10) by segment
3. **WTP Clustering:** Group willingness-to-pay signals into price bands
4. **Feature Demand Ranking:** Count feature requests by frequency

### Qualitative Analysis

1. **Affinity Mapping:** Print all quotes on sticky notes. Cluster by theme. Identify patterns.
2. **Persona Validation:** Compare proto-personas to real interview data. Update or retire personas.
3. **Journey Map Update:** Revise customer journey maps based on actual reported experiences.
4. **Insight Statements:** Write "We learned that..." statements for the top 10 findings.

### Decision Framework

| Finding | Action |
|---|---|
| ≥70% of a segment confirms the core pain at severity ≥7/10 | ✅ Proceed to MVP with this segment |
| 40-69% confirm pain at severity ≥7/10 | ⚠️ Investigate further — may need repositioning |
| <40% confirm pain at severity ≥7/10 | ❌ Kill or deprioritise this segment |
| ≥50% express WTP at our target price point | ✅ Price hypothesis validated |
| <50% express WTP at our target price point | ⚠️ Revise pricing or value proposition |
| New, unexpected pain point emerges in ≥30% of interviews | 🔄 Add to pain analysis, consider pivot |

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low recruitment rate for pharmacies | Medium | High | Increase walk-in attempts. Use LPC warm introductions. Offer to come back at a convenient time. |
| C2C sellers ghost scheduled calls | High | Medium | Over-recruit by 50%. Send reminders 24hrs and 1hr before. Have backup slots. |
| Students unavailable during summer | High | High | Adjust timeline to align with term dates. Target summer school students. |
| Interviewees give "polite" answers, not honest ones | Medium | High | Use "Mom Test" methodology. Focus on past behaviour, not hypotheticals. |
| Founder bias in interpreting results | Medium | High | Have a neutral observer. Use structured analysis framework. Count, don't just interpret. |
