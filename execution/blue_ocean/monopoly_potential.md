# Monopoly Potential: Peter Thiel's Seven Questions

## Framework

In *Zero to One*, Peter Thiel argues that every successful startup must answer seven questions. A truly great company — one that can build a monopoly in its market — must have a strong answer to most of them. Here is our honest assessment.

---

## The Seven Questions

### 1. The Engineering Question: Can You Create Breakthrough Technology — Not Just Incremental Improvements?

> *"Proprietary technology must be at least 10 times better than its closest substitute in some important dimension."*

**Our answer: YES — with nuance.**

| Dimension | Current Best | Our Offering | Improvement |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Trust cost** | £35-50/courier (DBS check) | £0 (courier self-funds stake) | **∞ (cost eliminated)** |
| **Onboarding time** | 5-10 business days | Instant (stake + go) | **50-100x faster** |
| **Chain-of-custody proof** | Photo proof (fakeable) | Cryptographic TOTP (irrefutable) | **10x more reliable** |
| **Courier take rate** | 60-65% (Stuart) | 85% | **1.3x better** |
| **Merchant onboarding** | Sales call + contract + integration | Self-service in <10 min | **10x faster** |

**10x assessment:**
- ✅ Trust cost: Infinite improvement (from something to nothing)
- ✅ Onboarding speed: 50-100x improvement
- ✅ Chain-of-custody: 10x improvement (cryptographic vs. photo)
- ❌ Courier take rate: Only 1.3x improvement (meaningful but not 10x)
- ✅ Merchant onboarding: 10x improvement

**Honest score: 7/10** — We have genuine 10x improvements in trust mechanics and onboarding, but not across every dimension. Our delivery speed, coverage, and basic UX are at parity, not 10x better.

**What would make it 10/10:** If the TOTP handshake protocol becomes an open standard adopted by multiple platforms, our engineering contribution becomes industry-defining — comparable to how Google's PageRank algorithm wasn't just a better search but a new paradigm for information retrieval.

### 2. The Timing Question: Is Now the Right Time to Start This Particular Business?

> *"The best opportunities are found in overlooked times and places."*

**Our answer: STRONG YES.**

Five conditions have converged to make this the optimal launch window:

| Condition | Status | Why Now |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Quick commerce collapse** (2023-2024) | Getir, Gorillas failed → market skepticism of "owned infrastructure" model | Investors are ready for the opposite thesis: own nothing, provide trust |
| **Post-COVID delivery normalization** | Permanent shift in consumer expectations | Local merchants now expect to offer delivery; addressable market is 5-10x pre-COVID |
| **Uber v Aslam precedent** (2021) | Clear legal framework for worker classification | We can architect True Marketplace with confidence in legal boundaries |
| **TOTP/QR familiarity** (2020+) | COVID check-ins, 2FA normalization | Users intuitively understand QR scanning — zero education cost |
| **Cost of living crisis** (2024-2026) | Students seeking flexible income; merchants seeking cost reductions | Both sides of marketplace are actively looking for what we offer |

**Honest score: 9/10** — The timing is excellent. We are launching into a window where every macro trend favors our model. The only risk is that the window might close if a well-funded competitor identifies the same opportunity — but given that the quick-commerce hangover is still fresh, we likely have 12-18 months before serious competition emerges.

### 3. The Monopoly Question: Are You Starting With a Big Share of a Small Market?

> *"A startup messed up at its foundation cannot be fixed. Start small and monopolize."*

**Our answer: STRONG YES.**

**Our initial market:** Same-day local delivery within the UCL/Bloomsbury wedge (2.5 km²).

| Market Dimension | Size | Our Target Share |
|-----------------|------|-----------------|
| Total merchants in wedge | ~120 | 80+ (66%) |
| Current same-day delivery users | ~25-35 | All of them |
| Non-users convertible | ~50-85 | 50% (25-43) |
| **Total addressable in wedge** | **~120** | **80+ (66%)** |

**Annual delivery volume in wedge:** ~50,000-100,000 deliveries
**Revenue from wedge:** £55,000-110,000/year

**Is this market small enough to monopolize?** Yes. A 2.5 km² zone with 120 merchants is small enough that one sales person (founder) can visit every merchant personally. Network effects at this scale create winner-take-most dynamics — there's room for exactly one local delivery platform with sufficient courier density.

**Is this market big enough to matter?** As a standalone market, no — £100K/year is not venture-scale. But as a beachhead from which to expand to 500+ university wedges globally, yes. Each wedge is a replicable unit with the same dynamics.

**Honest score: 9/10** — Classic Thiel-style small market domination. The only question is whether the wedge-to-wedge expansion maintains monopoly characteristics (we believe it does, due to hyper-local network effects).

### 4. The People Question: Do You Have the Right Team?

> *"A startup is only as good as its people. The founding team is everything."*

**Our answer: HONEST WEAKNESS.**

**Current team status:**

| Role | Filled? | Assessment |
|------|---------|------------|
| Founder / CEO | Yes | Technical founder with logistics domain knowledge; Innovator Founder Visa applicant |
| CTO / Lead Developer | ❌ No | Critical gap — need someone to build the TOTP protocol and marketplace platform |
| Operations Lead | ❌ No | Needed for merchant onboarding and courier operations |
| Legal Advisor | Partial | Access to employment law guidance but no dedicated counsel |
| Product Designer | ❌ No | MVP can launch with founder-designed UI but needs professional UX |

**Founding team gaps:**

The single-founder model is a known risk factor for investors. Key hires needed:

1. **Technical Co-Founder (Critical):** Must build TOTP protocol, marketplace matching, payment integration. Ideally has experience with two-sided marketplaces and cryptographic systems.

2. **Operations Hire (Important):** First non-technical hire to manage merchant onboarding, courier support, and day-to-day operations. Ideally a UCL student or recent graduate embedded in the community.

3. **Legal Counsel (Important):** Employment law specialist to validate True Marketplace architecture and prepare for potential tribunal challenges.

**Honest score: 4/10** — This is our weakest dimension. A single founder without a technical co-founder is a significant risk. Investors will push back here. The mitigation is: (a) demonstrate rapid MVP development with contracted developers, (b) use the Innovator Founder Visa endorsement process to validate the founder's capability, (c) actively recruit a technical co-founder during the pre-seed phase.

### 5. The Distribution Question: Do You Have a Way to Not Just Create but Deliver Your Product?

> *"Superior products rarely win by themselves. Distribution is everything."*

**Our answer: YES — through structural distribution advantages.**

**Our distribution strategy:**

| Channel | Mechanism | CAC | Scalability |
|---------|-----------|-----|-------------|
| **Courier-as-salesperson** | Every courier pickup is a merchant touch-point; couriers naturally promote to non-client merchants | £0 | High (scales with courier count) |
| **University partnerships** | Student union endorsement, society partnerships, campus ambassadors | £2-5/merchant | Medium (one partnership per university) |
| **Merchant referral** | £10 credit for merchant-to-merchant referral | £10/merchant | High (word-of-mouth in dense zones) |
| **Walking demos** | Founder/ops person walks the high street, demos to each merchant in person | £0 (time cost only) | Low (but effective for first 30 merchants) |
| **Local press/blogs** | "UCL students launch delivery platform" — local news angle | £0 | Medium (one-time boost) |

**Why distribution works for us:**

1. **Geographic density = free distribution.** In a 2.5 km² zone, every merchant knows every other merchant. One successful delivery generates word-of-mouth to 5 neighboring shops.

2. **Product IS distribution.** The courier wearing "LocalLogistics" carries our brand to every merchant and recipient. Every delivery is an advertisement.

3. **No enterprise sales needed.** We sell a £49/month SaaS product to independent merchants. This is a self-service purchase, not a 6-month enterprise sales cycle.

**Honest score: 7/10** — Strong distribution mechanics for the beachhead wedge. The question is whether these translate to new wedges without founder presence. We need to prove that the playbook (courier-as-salesperson + university partnership) is replicable without the founder visiting every shop.

### 6. The Durability Question: Will Your Market Position Be Defensible 10 and 20 Years into the Future?

> *"You must think about whether your business will still be around a decade from now."*

**Our answer: YES — if we achieve protocol standardization.**

**10-year defensibility assessment:**

| Threat | Probability | Our Defense | Durability |
|--------|------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Uber/Deliveroo enters local logistics** | High (60%) | They can't adopt staking (contradicts employer model); see defensibility.md | Strong |
| **New startup copies our model** | Medium (40%) | Network effects + data moat + brand in each wedge | Moderate-Strong |
| **Technology becomes commodity** | Medium (30%) | TOTP protocol as open standard positions us as the originator | Strong |
| **Regulation kills staking model** | Low (15%) | FCA staking compliance + proactive regulatory engagement | Moderate |
| **Market shifts away from local delivery** | Low (10%) | Post-COVID behavioral shift is permanent; local delivery is structural | Strong |
| **AI/autonomous delivery** | Medium (30%) in 10yr | We're vehicle-agnostic; autonomous robots use same protocol | Strong |

**20-year vision:**

In 20 years, Local-Logistics Protocol is either:
1. **The TCP/IP of physical delivery** — our TOTP handshake is the global standard for chain-of-custody verification, used by every delivery platform. We are the infrastructure layer, not the application layer.
2. **The dominant local delivery platform** in 50+ cities globally, with unassailable network effects in each.
3. **Acquired by Amazon/Uber/FedEx** for our protocol IP and established network — a successful exit.

**Honest score: 6/10** — Durability depends heavily on execution of the protocol standardization strategy. Without it, we're a well-run local marketplace — defensible but not transformative. With protocol standardization, we're building 20-year infrastructure.

### 7. The Secret Question: Have You Identified a Unique Opportunity That Others Don't See?

> *"Every great business is built around a secret that's hidden from the outside."*

**Our answer: STRONG YES.**

**Our secret:**

> **Background checks are a tax on trust, not a source of trust. Economic skin-in-the-game is a superior trust mechanism — cheaper, faster, and more reliable. The entire logistics industry is paying billions for inferior trust.**

This secret is hidden because:

1. **Incumbents can't see it:** They've invested billions in background check infrastructure, fleet management, and HR systems. Admitting that a £50 deposit could replace all of that would mean admitting their core investment is worthless.

2. **Regulators haven't considered it:** Employment law focuses on the relationship between platform and worker, not on alternative trust mechanisms. No regulator has evaluated whether economic staking could replace vetting.

3. **Workers don't know it's possible:** Gig workers have been conditioned to accept background checks, training programs, and onboarding processes as the cost of entry. Nobody has offered them "deposit money and start immediately."

4. **Investors haven't connected the dots:** The staking mechanism is familiar from crypto/DeFi but hasn't been applied to physical logistics. Crypto investors don't think about delivery; logistics investors don't think about staking.

**Why this secret is contrarian but correct:**

| Conventional Wisdom | Our Contrarian Belief | Evidence |
|--------------------|----------------------|----------|
| "You need background checks for trust" | "You need skin-in-the-game for trust" | Security deposits work for housing, crypto staking works for blockchains — economic alignment is proven |
| "Delivery platforms need fleet vehicles" | "The best fleet is no fleet — people on foot in dense zones" | Quick commerce failure proved fleet ownership destroys unit economics |
| "Gig workers need to be classified and managed" | "Protocol participants need incentive alignment, not management" | Uber v Aslam shows management creates legal liability; our model avoids it entirely |
| "Logistics is a scale business — go big or go home" | "Logistics is a density business — go deep or go home" | Every failed same-day delivery startup expanded too fast and couldn't maintain density |

**Honest score: 8/10** — Our secret is genuinely contrarian, supported by evidence from adjacent domains (crypto, housing, insurance), and structurally invisible to incumbents. The only reason it's not 10/10 is that the secret could be discovered and replicated by a well-funded startup before we establish defensibility.
