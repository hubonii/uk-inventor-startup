## 2. Sales Process

### 2.1 Sales Funnel

```
Research (100 merchants mapped)
    │
    ▼
Cold Outreach (40 in-person visits)
    │
    ▼
First Meeting (25 interested)
    │
    ▼
Demo / Trial Discussion (20 engaged)
    │
    ▼
LOI / Verbal Commit (18 commits)
    │
    ▼
Onboarded & First Drop (15 live)
    │
    ▼
Monthly Active Merchant (15 retained)
```

**Expected conversion rates:**
- Cold visit → Meeting: 60%
- Meeting → Demo: 80%
- Demo → LOI: 90%
- LOI → Onboarded: 85%
- **Overall: 40 visits → 15 live merchants**

### 2.2 Outreach Protocol

**Method: In-person, door-to-door, during quiet hours (10:00–11:30 AM or 2:30–4:00 PM)**

**Step 1 — The Drop-In (5 minutes)**
1. Walk in during a quiet period
2. Introduce yourself: "Hi, I'm [Name], I'm building a delivery service specifically for local shops like yours"
3. Ask: "Do your customers ever ask if you deliver?"
4. Leave a one-page info sheet and your business card
5. Ask: "Could I come back for 15 minutes this week to show you how it works?"

**Step 2 — The Demo Meeting (15 minutes)**
1. Show the merchant app on your phone
2. Walk through: "A customer places an order → you tap 'dispatch' → a local courier arrives in 8 minutes → they scan the QR code → deliver to the customer → customer confirms with their QR code"
3. Emphasise: "The courier deposits £50 before they can carry anything. If they lose the package, you're covered."
4. Show pricing: "£49/month for the dashboard + 15% of each delivery fee. Your customer pays for delivery, not you."
5. Ask: "What would 10 deliveries a week mean for your business?"

**Step 3 — The Close (5 minutes)**
1. "We're launching with [X] other shops in Bloomsbury next month"
2. "The first 3 months are free — no subscription, no platform fee. You're helping us build the product."
3. "All I need is 15 minutes with one of your staff to set up the app"
4. Get verbal yes → schedule onboarding

### 2.3 Sales Script — Pharmacy Version

> **Opener:** "Hi, I'm [Name]. I'm working with pharmacies in Bloomsbury to offer 20-minute prescription delivery to their customers. Do you currently offer any delivery service?"
>
> **Problem agitation:** "I've spoken to a few pharmacy owners around here, and they all say the same thing — customers ask for delivery, but hiring a driver doesn't make sense for 5-10 deliveries a day. And services like Deliveroo aren't set up for prescriptions."
>
> **Solution:** "We've built a platform that connects you with local couriers — mostly UCL students — who can pick up and deliver within 20 minutes. The clever bit is the security: every courier puts down a £50 deposit, and every handoff uses a QR code — like a digital chain of custody. So you know exactly who has the package and when it was delivered."
>
> **Social proof:** "We're starting with [X] other pharmacies in the area — [Name] on [Street] has already signed up."
>
> **Pricing:** "It's £49 a month for the dashboard — which shows you real-time tracking, delivery history, and customer analytics. We take 15% of the delivery fee, which your customer pays. So your cost is just the monthly subscription."
>
> **Close:** "We're doing free setup this month, and the first 3 months are on us. Could I come back on [day] for 15 minutes to get you set up?"

## 3. Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| **"We already use Royal Mail / DPD"** | "That's great for next-day. We're for the customer who says 'I need this in 20 minutes.' We're the urgent option alongside your existing setup." |
| **"How do I know the courier won't steal the package?"** | "Every courier deposits £50 before they can carry anything. If a package goes missing, you're compensated from that deposit immediately. Plus, every handoff is cryptographically verified — we know exactly who had the package and when." |
| **"It's too expensive"** | "The £49/month pays for itself if you get just 2 extra customers a week who otherwise would've gone to a competitor with delivery. And the delivery fee is paid by the customer, not you." |
| **"My customers won't use it"** | "We hear that a lot. Here's what we've seen: once you put up a small sign saying 'We deliver in 20 minutes,' customers ask about it. We provide the signage for free." |
| **"I don't have time to deal with another app"** | "Setup takes 15 minutes. After that, it's one tap per delivery. We'll train your team and you have my personal mobile number for any issues." |
| **"What about liability / insurance?"** | "We carry goods-in-transit insurance up to £500 per package. The courier's £50 deposit covers smaller claims instantly. And our QR chain-of-custody protocol creates a complete legal audit trail." |
| **"What if the courier is late or doesn't show?"** | "If a courier doesn't arrive within 10 minutes of acceptance, we automatically reassign. You can see real-time courier location in the app. Average delivery time is 18 minutes." |
| **"I need to talk to my business partner / head office"** | "Absolutely. Can I leave you this one-pager and my card? I'll pop back on [day]. Or if it's easier, I can do a quick 10-minute video call with both of you." |
| **"We tried delivery before and it didn't work"** | "What happened? [Listen]. The difference here is you don't hire anyone, you don't manage anyone. You tap one button and a verified courier appears. Zero operational overhead." |

## 7. Vertical-Specific Playbooks

### 7.1 Pharmacy Playbook

**Why pharmacies win:**
- Prescriptions are **urgent** — patients can't wait for next-day delivery
- High trust requirement — chain-of-custody protocol is a key differentiator
- NHS community pharmacy contract encourages local delivery services
- Average basket: £15–40 (prescription + OTC items)
- Repeat customers: prescription refills create recurring delivery demand

**Pharmacy-specific pitch points:**
1. "TOTP QR handshake creates a tamper-proof audit trail — important for prescription accountability"
2. "Our couriers are background-checked and stake a £50 deposit — suitable for handling medication"
3. "We can integrate with your existing dispensing workflow — dispatch after bagging"
4. "NHS patients who can't collect prescriptions can now get same-hour delivery"

**Compliance considerations:**
- Prescriptions must be bagged and sealed before courier collection
- Courier never opens or inspects prescription contents
- Temperature-sensitive medications flagged and time-limited (30 min max delivery)
- Controlled drugs excluded from courier delivery (regulatory requirement)

### 7.2 Print Shop Playbook

**Why print shops win:**
- Students urgently need printed dissertations, posters, business cards
- "Send to print and get it delivered" removes a trip to the shop
- Seasonal spikes: exam season, dissertation deadlines, freshers' week
- Average order: £8–25

**Print-specific pitch points:**
1. "Students upload to your website, you print it, tap 'dispatch' — delivered to their door in 20 minutes"
2. "During dissertation season, you could do 30+ deliveries a day"
3. "You keep 100% of print revenue — delivery fee paid by the student"

### 7.3 Convenience / Hardware Playbook

**Why convenience stores:**
- Impulse purchases: "I need batteries / a phone charger / paracetamol NOW"
- Competes with Amazon Prime Now on speed (20 min vs 2 hours)
- Average basket: £5–15
- High frequency: potential for daily orders from halls of residence

**Convenience-specific pitch points:**
1. "A student in halls needs paracetamol at 9 PM — they order from your shop and get it in 15 minutes"
2. "You become the 'corner shop that delivers' — none of your competitors do this"
3. "No minimum order — even a £3 item is worth delivering because the customer pays the delivery fee"
