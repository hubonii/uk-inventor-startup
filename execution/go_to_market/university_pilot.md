# UCL University Pilot Plan — Local-Logistics Protocol

## Pilot Summary

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **University** | University College London (UCL) |
| **Duration** | 8 weeks (expandable to 12 if metrics positive) |
| **Geography** | 1-mile radius from UCL Main Quad, Gower Street |
| **Target drops** | 100/week by Week 4; 250/week by Week 8 |
| **Pilot type** | Live commercial operations (not simulation) |
| **Revenue model** | Full pricing active (15% platform fee + per-drop courier fee) |

---

## 1. Why UCL — Strategic Rationale

### 1.1 Demographic Density
- **43,000+ students** in a 0.5 km² campus area
- **12 halls of residence** within 1-mile radius (6,000+ bed spaces)
- Student population skews **tech-savvy, mobile-first, cost-conscious**
- High package volume: students order online but struggle with parcel collection

### 1.2 Merchant Density
- **23 pharmacies** within 1 mile of UCL Main Quad
- **8 print/copy shops** serving student essay and dissertation printing
- **15+ independent cafés and food shops** with delivery potential
- **3 hardware/convenience stores** on Tottenham Court Road and Goodge Street

### 1.3 Courier Supply
- Large population of students seeking **flexible part-time work**
- Walkable/cyclable campus — no vehicle needed
- Sports club members (running, cycling) are ideal early adopters
- UCL has a **strong entrepreneurship culture** (UCL Hatchery, UCLU Entrepreneurship Society)

### 1.4 Institutional Support
- UCL Innovation & Enterprise actively supports student ventures
- UCL has existing relationships with local businesses via Town-Gown initiatives
- Potential to position pilot as "student employment research project"

### 3.2 Shift Structure

**Operating Hours:** 09:00–21:00, 7 days/week

| Shift | Hours | Courier Slots | Expected Demand |
|-------|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| Morning | 09:00–13:00 | 3–5 couriers | Low (pharmacy runs, print jobs) |
| Afternoon Peak | 13:00–17:00 | 5–8 couriers | High (lunch break errands, merchant dispatch) |
| Evening | 17:00–21:00 | 4–6 couriers | Medium (after-class pickups, hall deliveries) |

### 3.3 Guaranteed Earnings Model (Weeks 1–4 Only)

To bootstrap supply before demand materialises:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Guaranteed rate | £12/hr during scheduled shifts |
| Minimum shift length | 2 hours |
| Maximum guaranteed hours/week/courier | 8 hours |
| Total weekly guarantee budget | £12 × 8 hrs × 25 couriers = £2,400 |
| Offset by actual earnings | Courier keeps all drop fees; guarantee covers shortfall only |
| Expected actual shortfall | £800–1,200/week (based on projected drop volume) |

**Example:** Courier works 4-hour shift, completes 3 drops earning £9 total. Guaranteed earnings = £48. Platform pays £39 top-up.

**Exit from guarantee:** When average courier earns ≥ £12/hr organically (expected by Week 5–6), guarantee is quietly retired.

### 3.4 Courier Onboarding Process

```
Step 1: Application (5 min)
  └─→ Online form: name, phone, UCL affiliation, availability

Step 2: KYC Verification (24 hrs)
  └─→ Onfido: ID scan + selfie match + right-to-work check

Step 3: Onboarding Session (60 min)
  └─→ In-person at UCL Hatchery or video call
  └─→ Content: app walkthrough, TOTP protocol training, safety briefing, earnings model

Step 4: Shadowed First Drop (30 min)
  └─→ Paired with experienced courier or founder for first live delivery

Step 5: Solo First Drop (15 min)
  └─→ Complete first independent delivery with real-time support chat

Step 6: Staking Unlock (after 5 completed drops)
  └─→ Courier deposits £50 stake to unlock premium features and priority matching
  └─→ Waived for first 2 weeks during beta
```

## 5. Operational Playbook

### 5.1 Daily Operations Schedule

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 08:30 | Ops check: courier availability for day, any merchant issues |
| 09:00 | Platform goes live; morning couriers sign in |
| 12:00 | Midday check: drop volume, courier utilisation, any stuck deliveries |
| 13:00 | Afternoon shift couriers sign in |
| 17:00 | Evening shift transition; review afternoon metrics |
| 21:00 | Platform pauses new requests; in-flight drops complete |
| 21:30 | End-of-day report: drops completed, issues, earnings summary |

### 5.2 Incident Response Protocol

| Incident | Response | SLA |
|----------|----------|-----|
| TOTP handshake failure | Ops manually verifies via phone; overrides in backend | 5 min |
| Courier no-show after accepting | Reassign to next available courier; original courier flagged | 3 min |
| Package damaged | Photo evidence collected; staking pool claim if courier fault | 24 hrs |
| Package lost | Immediate investigation; courier stake frozen; sender refund | 4 hrs |
| Customer complaint | Founder calls within 2 hours; log in incident tracker | 2 hrs |
| App outage | Engineering on-call; status page updated; SMS to active couriers | 15 min |

### 5.3 Weekly Merchant Check-ins

Every merchant receives a **15-minute weekly visit** from the founder or ops lead:
1. Review past week's drop volume and any issues
2. Collect feedback on courier quality and timing
3. Discuss upcoming busy periods or special needs
4. Ensure signage is displayed and counter cards are stocked
5. Quick demo of any new dashboard features

## 8. Post-Pilot Decision Framework

### Scenario A: Pilot Succeeds (All Primary KPIs Met)
→ Expand to full 1-mile Bloomsbury zone
→ Begin merchant acquisition in adjacent high streets (Tottenham Court Road south, Lamb's Conduit Street)
→ Prepare seed round materials with UCL pilot data

### Scenario B: Partial Success (≥ 4 of 7 Primary KPIs Met)
→ Extend pilot 4 weeks with targeted interventions on failing metrics
→ Increase investment in weakest area (supply or demand)
→ Re-evaluate at Week 12

### Scenario C: Pilot Fails (< 4 Primary KPIs Met)
→ Conduct post-mortem: is it product, market, or execution failure?
→ If product: fix and re-run 4-week mini-pilot
→ If market: consider adjacent use case (e.g., B2B only, no consumer)
→ If execution: bring in operational co-founder

### Scenario D: Breakthrough Success (All KPIs exceeded by > 50%)
→ Immediately open waitlist for adjacent zones (King's Cross, Islington)
→ Accelerate fundraise timeline
→ Hire first full-time ops manager
