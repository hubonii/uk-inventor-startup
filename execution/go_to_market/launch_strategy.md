# Launch Strategy — Local-Logistics Protocol

## Executive Summary

Local-Logistics Protocol will execute a **three-phase launch** designed to achieve product-market fit in a single hyper-dense geographic wedge (UCL / Bloomsbury) before expanding. Every phase has hard entry/exit criteria, a capped budget, and a kill switch if fundamentals break.

---

## Phase 0 — Pre-Launch (Weeks –12 to 0)

### Objective
Build a waitlist of ≥ 500 interested senders/recipients and secure ≥ 10 signed merchant LOIs before writing a single line of production courier-matching code.

### Workstreams

| # | Workstream | Owner | Deliverable | Deadline |
|---|-----------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Landing page + waitlist | Growth | Convertkit page, "Be First" signup | Week –12 |
| 2 | University partnerships | BD | Signed MOU with UCL Student Union, Halls Office | Week –10 |
| 3 | Merchant LOIs | BD | 10 signed Letters of Intent from pharmacies/shops | Week –6 |
| 4 | Anchor courier pool | Ops | 30 pre-registered couriers (KYC complete) | Week –4 |
| 5 | Press & influencer seeding | Marketing | Embargo briefings with TechCrunch UK, Sifted, UKTN | Week –2 |
| 6 | Beta invite emails | Growth | Drip sequence: 5 emails over 14 days | Week –1 |

### Waitlist Growth Tactics
- **UCL Freshers' Fair booth** — QR code to waitlist, raffle a £100 Amazon voucher (cost: £150 all-in)
- **Student Union digital screens** — 30-second loop: "Send anything across campus in 20 minutes"
- **Pharmacy counter cards** — A5 tent cards: "Coming Soon: On-demand delivery for your prescriptions"
- **Reddit / r/UCL + r/london** — Organic posts from founder account; no astroturfing
- **LinkedIn thought-leadership** — Founder publishes 3 articles on "trust infrastructure for local logistics"

### University Partnership Approach
1. **UCL Innovation & Enterprise** — Position as "student-founded logistics research project"; request sandbox support
2. **UCL Student Union** — Propose pilot as a "student employment scheme" (couriers earn £12+/hr)
3. **Halls of Residence** — Negotiate parcel-room access for QR handoff (Ramsay Hall, Campbell House, Astor College)
4. **UCL Estates** — Secure permission for couriers to access campus buildings during operating hours (08:00–22:00)

## Phase 1 — Soft Launch (Weeks 1–6)

### Objective
Validate core product loop with **50 active senders** and **20 active couriers** in a 0.5-mile radius around UCL Main Campus. Prove that the cryptographic chain-of-custody protocol works in the wild.

### Geographic Boundary
- **Centre:** UCL Main Quad (51.5246° N, 0.1340° W)
- **Radius:** 0.5 miles (≈ 800 m)
- **Coverage:** Bloomsbury, northern Fitzrovia, eastern Marylebone
- **Boundary enforcement:** App rejects pickup/drop-off pins outside polygon

### Launch Sequence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 1 | Invite first 50 waitlist users (highest engagement score) |
| Day 1 | Activate 20 pre-registered couriers |
| Day 1 | Onboard 5 anchor merchants (pharmacies) |
| Day 3 | First "real" delivery; founder shadows courier |
| Day 7 | Collect first NPS scores |
| Day 14 | Review unit economics; adjust pricing if margin < £0.30/drop |
| Day 21 | Second cohort invite (next 50 users) |
| Day 28 | Merchant cohort 2 (5 more merchants) |
| Day 42 | Soft Launch review — go / no-go for Hard Launch |

### Success Criteria — Soft Launch Exit

| Metric | Target | Kill Switch |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Weekly active senders | ≥ 50 | < 20 for 2 consecutive weeks |
| Weekly drops completed | ≥ 100 | < 40 |
| Delivery success rate | ≥ 95% | < 85% |
| Average delivery time (door-to-door) | ≤ 25 min | > 45 min |
| Sender NPS | ≥ 40 | < 10 |
| Courier NPS | ≥ 30 | < 0 |
| Gross margin per drop | ≥ £0.30 | Negative for 2 consecutive weeks |
| Custody disputes (TOTP failures) | < 2% of drops | > 5% |
| Staking incidents (fraud/loss) | 0 | ≥ 2 incidents |

## Contingency Plans

### Contingency A — Demand Exists but Supply Insufficient
- **Trigger:** > 50% of drops have wait time > 30 min due to no available couriers
- **Response:** Activate "guaranteed earnings" mode — pay couriers £10/hr flat for 2-week sprint
- **Budget reserve:** £2,000
- **Duration:** 2 weeks max, then reassess pricing

### Contingency B — Supply Exists but Demand Insufficient
- **Trigger:** Courier utilisation < 1 drop/hour during peak
- **Response:** Slash sender fees by 30% for 4 weeks; run "First 3 drops free" campaign
- **Budget reserve:** £1,500
- **Duration:** 4 weeks, then reassess product-market fit

### Contingency C — Trust Protocol Failures
- **Trigger:** > 3 disputed handoffs in any single week; staking pool claims > £200
- **Response:** Pause new signups; mandate in-person founder shadowing of every 5th drop; patch TOTP flow
- **Budget reserve:** £0 (engineering time only)
- **Duration:** Until dispute rate < 1% for 2 consecutive weeks

### Contingency D — Regulatory Challenge
- **Trigger:** HMRC or local council questions courier employment status
- **Response:** Activate pre-drafted legal opinion (True Marketplace defence); engage employment law counsel
- **Budget reserve:** £3,000 (legal retainer)
- **Duration:** Ongoing until resolved

### Contingency E — Full Abort
- **Trigger:** Negative gross margin for 4 consecutive weeks AND sender NPS < 0 AND < 20 weekly drops
- **Response:** Wind down operations; refund all courier stakes; publish post-mortem
- **Budget reserve:** £2,500 (stake refunds + communications)
- **Timeline:** 2-week wind-down
