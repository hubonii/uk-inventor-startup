# Problem Statement

Urban logistics faces a trilemma: speed, cost, and environmental impact. Current solutions force compromises and rely on outdated, highly polluting, and economically inefficient models.

## 1. Environmental Impact
The explosion of e-commerce has flooded cities with delivery vans, leading to severe congestion and high carbon emissions. Traditional "last-mile" delivery is the least efficient and most polluting segment of the supply chain.

## 2. Inefficient Use of Human Capital
Millions of commuters travel across the city every day with empty hands or half-empty backpacks. This massive volume of daily, predictable human transit is completely unmonetized and ignored by current logistics networks.

## 3. The "Gig Economy" Burnout
Traditional platforms (like Uber or Deliveroo) force couriers to chase randomized, unpredictable orders, creating a stressful, gamified environment. This leads to high churn rates and dissatisfaction among gig workers.

## 4. Security & Trust Deficit in P2P
Existing attempts at peer-to-peer delivery lack bulletproof trust. Senders fear their items will be stolen, and couriers fear being unwitting mules for contraband.
